Ways to improve your speaking

Meetup Groups

Join a Meetup Group in your area. You're bound to find like-minded people that you share things in common with. This is absolutely the best way to improve your English - by speaking it with other people in real-life situations.

Ways to improve your listening

Podcasts

Podcasts are awesome. The ones you'll find on following websites are for Learners of English, so they are short and very focused. You can download them and listen to them on your phone. Highly recommended!

  • http://www.bbc.co.uk/podcasts/series/tae (British English)
  • http://learningenglish.voanews.com/section/level-one/3774.html (American English)

TED Talks

TED (Technology, Education and Design) is a nonprofit group devoted to spreading ideas, usually in the form of short, powerful talks (18 minutes or less). Just browse through the listings and choose ones that are interesting. You can turn the subtitles on if you need them. http://www.ted.com/talks/browse

Watch TV!

Students always ask me to recommend good films to help improve their English. And I always say, you should be following a TV series! For one thing, they're shorter (30 minutes to one hour), so you'll be more likely to watch one every day or every other day. Not many of us have time to watch several films a week. I also like TV series because you are likely to hear the same kind of language repeated over and over, and it will stick in your mind.

Three series I really like, because they are interesting and pretty easy to follow, are The Good Wife, Suits and Lie to Me. You can find them on Netflix.

  • The Good Wife: http://www.imdb.com/title/tt1442462/
  • Suits: http://www.imdb.com/title/tt1632701/
  • Lie to Me: http://www.imdb.com/title/tt1235099/

It's okay to watch with subtitles, in fact, I recommend it! The point with watching TV series is to watch them "fluently" the way you would in your own language. And if you don't know what they're saying, then it won't be fun. Over time, as you get to know the characters, try turning the subtitles off. Another good exercise is to watch an episode once with subtitles, and then repeat it a day or two later with the subtitles turned off.

Keeping a vocabulary notebook

It's also nice to keep your vocabulary notebook with you while you're watching. You don't need to write down every word you don't know, but you can have it there in case you want to jot something down. Don't stop watching to look up a word, unless you really need it. Get in the habit of organizing your notebook by writing the show and the episode on the top of your page, then the word and what time it appeared on screen. That way if you want to go back, it's easy to find.

Ways to improve your reading and vocabulary

Mobile Apps

Read, read, read... every single day. Make sure your phone's settings are in English, and download apps that will encourage you to read in English when you have a few free minutes and find yourself looking at your phone. Since I'm in Dublin, I always try to encourage my students to read Irish news sites.

http://www.rte.ie/extra/app/news-now/

http://m.irishtimes.com/holding.html

If you are from Spain and want to keep up with the Spanish news in English, El PAIS offers quite a few articles in English every day. These are translated from the Spanish original, but the translations are done by highly qualified native speakers, so they are very good.

http://elpais.com/elpais/inenglish.html

Check out graded readers from your local library

Get a library card, and then ask the librarian to help you find the OXFORD BOOKWORMS or PENGUIN READERS books. They are organised by level; make sure you get one that's right for you. You'll be able to read them pretty quickly, and you'll feel a sense of accomplishment when you finish.
